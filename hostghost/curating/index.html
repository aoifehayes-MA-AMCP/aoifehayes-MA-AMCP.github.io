Curating a plan of a plan

· Aoife Hayes

Detailed here are the practical elements of curating a plan of a plan. Sensitive information such as budget and contract are included in full in the appendices accompanying the introduction to this portfolio.

host/ghost commission budget

Lily O’Shea was paid an artist fee for her work. Installation costs were low and the venue cost was split between the three producers occupying St Peter’s.

Contract

A letter of agreement was issued to Lily O’Shea on 30th March 2022. This contract confirmed O’Shea’s agreement to create new work for host/ghost in response to the issued commission brief.

This letter also provided details of the installation timeline and exhibition run and confirmed the agreed fee.

Installation

The installation of a plan of a plan was quick and simple – perhaps partly because it had to be. Installation consumed 15 minutes of Lily’s 30 minute lunchbreak from her fulltime job in retail.

Meetings & Check-ins

Admittedly, my liaison and interaction with the artist was minimal during the production process. While this was not the original intention, the temporality of producing within a larger festival and academic framework made time scarce and contact infrequent.

However, as a result, Lily was afforded agency over her time and labour and was not subjugated to constant deadlines, self-assessments or displays of productivity.
