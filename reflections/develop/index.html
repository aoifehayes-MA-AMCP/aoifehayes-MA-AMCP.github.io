How Has My Practice Developed

· Aoife Hayes

Reflecting on these initial journal entries now, I am confident that I respected the intentions set at the beginning of the academic year. While my core values remain the same, my research interests have undergone a transformation. I believe this shift to be a result of the specific temporal cross contamination of retail work, academia and curatorial practice.

When setting out to co-produce host/ghost, I was primarily concerned with the urban environment, its neglection and how it might be better cared for in the future.

As I became familiar with theoretical frameworks regarding precarity, chronopolitics and temporalities, my attention shifted from concepts of place-making towards the identification of a sustainable approach to curation and arts production.

The value of co-curating becomes apparent here. I was afforded the agency to explore my research interests and various approaches to curation through the commissioning of a plan of a plan, while my co-producer Glenn was able to explore his own research interests and demonstrate the tangible effects of local urban dereliciton.
