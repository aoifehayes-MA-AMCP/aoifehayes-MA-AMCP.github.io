Crush

21st June – 26th April 2022, First Floor Corridor Backwater Artists Group

“Having a ‘crush’ is about texture…My surface gets all uneven, my underneath shows through, things shine up suddenly. It’s like ‘being’ crushed material, but also like wearing it, alternately slithery and itchy” – Elizabeth Freeman

Crush is an exhibition of work by four Backwater Artists Group studio members that demonstrates a nuance of textures. The exhibition explores everyday life through the materiality of texture, conveying transactions between objects, people, landscapes and labour over time. Texture can be visualised as wear and tear in the form of smears, stains and cracks but can also include everyday transformations like cooking, gardening, playing pretend or having a crush.

host/ghost

7th – 9th April 2022, St Peter’s Cork

host/ghost is a group exhibition curated by Aoife Hayes and M. Glenn Dunlea as part of the MA in Arts Management & Creative Producing programme at the School of Film, Music & Theatre, UCC.

host/ghost presents an exhibition of works by four emerging Cork-based artists.

Aoife Hayes commissioned a plan of a plan by Lily O’Shea which is an unfinished artwork that is based on the structure of a staircase. It is accompanied by a text titled ‘life in a project: a time of no time’ which explores the relationship between project work and precarity. The research process involved in this work focuses on the pressures surrounding creative pursuit and the issue of affording the time and space to maintain a sustainable art practice.
